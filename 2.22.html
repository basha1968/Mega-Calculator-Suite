What is half-life?
Half-life is the time required for a quantity to reduce to half its initial value.

Common Examples:
• Carbon-14: 5,730 years (radiocarbon dating)
• Uranium-238: 4.5 billion years
• Iodine-131: 8 days (medical use)

Calculation Formula:
N = N₀ × (1/2)t/T
Where:
N = Remaining quantity
N₀ = Initial quantity
t = Time elapsed
T = Half-life duration
